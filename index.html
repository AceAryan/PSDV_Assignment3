Topic: Distributions in PyMC

Introduction

  • Overview

PyMC is a powerful probabilistic programming library in Python, primarily used for Bayesian statistical modeling and inference. It provides an intuitive framework for defining and estimating complex probabilistic models using Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) and Variational Inference methods. One of the key components of PyMC is its extensive support for probability distributions, which serve as the foundation for defining stochastic variables in Bayesian models.

  • Purpose of Distributions in PyMC

Distributions in PyMC allow users to model uncertainty and incorporate prior knowledge into probabilistic models. These distributions can represent observed data, latent variables, or priors in a Bayesian framework. PyMC provides a wide range of continuous and discrete distributions, including Normal, Beta, Gamma, Poisson, and Bernoulli, among others. By leveraging these distributions, users can construct hierarchical models, perform Bayesian inference, and generate predictive samples.
