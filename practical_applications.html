Practical Applications of Distributions in PyMC

  • Medical Diagnosis: PyMC is used in disease prediction by modeling uncertainty in symptoms and test results. For example, Bayesian networks can estimate the probability of a disease given symptoms.
  • Financial Risk Analysis: In finance, PyMC helps in portfolio risk estimation by modeling stock returns using distributions like Normal or Student-T.
  • Marketing and A/B Testing: Businesses use Bayesian A/B testing with PyMC to analyze conversion rates and optimize marketing campaigns.
  • Manufacturing and Quality Control: PyMC distributions help detect anomalies in production lines by modeling product defects and process variability.
  • Sports Analytics: Used to predict player performance using Bayesian hierarchical models, improving scouting and game strategies.
  • Environmental Science: PyMC models climate change patterns and estimates uncertainties in weather predictions.

Example: Bayesian A/B Testing

This example models two versions of a webpage (A & B) and estimates which one has a better conversion rate using Beta and Binomial distributions.


import pymc as pm

with pm.Model():
    # Prior beliefs for conversion rates
    conversion_A = pm.Beta("conversion_A", alpha=2, beta=5)
    conversion_B = pm.Beta("conversion_B", alpha=2, beta=5)
    
    # Simulating observations
    obs_A = pm.Binomial("obs_A", n=100, p=conversion_A, observed=30)
    obs_B = pm.Binomial("obs_B", n=100, p=conversion_B, observed=35)

    trace = pm.sample(1000)
